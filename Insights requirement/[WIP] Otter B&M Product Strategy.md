# Otter SMB B\&M Product Strategy Brief

# Executive Summary

This document serves to outline our product strategy for the next 2-3 quarters in our quest to be a leader in the restaurant technology space. The investment areas include further enhancing our core [unified commerce platform](https://stripe.com/guides/unified-commerce-guide), leveraging AI for advanced insights unlocking our *mission control vision* and addressing key industry painpoints.

Our primary focus would be on further integrating our first party and 3rd party online ordering, POS, Kiosk, KDS and Loyalty into the unified commerce platform to unlock seamless, data-driven experiences for both restaurant operators and their customers. In pursuit of this our secondary focus will be exploring adjacencies to our core offerings and making build vs buy decisions to accelerate our progress.

When approaching this secondary focus emphasis will be put on expanding our portfolio in areas that drive incremental MRR. Expanding wallet share through up front package selling and post sale in product upsells will drive profitability and LTV while keeping CAC in check. That said it will be very important to ensure we do not drive incremental support costs with the expanded portfolio.

# Market Analysis

Otter’s strategy remains focused on the “Limited Service Segment” which remains the fastest growing segment by sales in 2024\. With that in mind here are some key indicators the industry is seeing:

1. The restaurant industry is forecast to reach $1.1 trillion in sales in 2024, with 1M+ outlets and 15.7M employees.  
2. 90% of restaurant operators say their customers are more value-conscious than before.  
3. 76% of operators say technology gives them a competitive edge.  
4. 45% of operators need more employees to support customer demand.  
5. 76% of operators report higher food costs in 2023 compared to 2022\.  
6. 81% of consumers not currently enrolled would likely join a loyalty program if offered.  
7. Nearly 8 in 10 operators expect their 2024 sales to be higher or hold steady compared to 2023\.  
8. 45% of operators expect more intense competition in 2024\.

1. Further enhance Unified Commerce Platform  
2. Develop AI-Driven Insights for operational efficiency, cost management, and improved order accuracy with a lens on increasing labor challenges.  
3. Doubledown on loyalty programs via 1st party solutions and integrations.

## Unified Commerce Platform

Moving from omni channel to a unified commerce platform means centralization of the restaurant data to create a platform that helps operators optimize their operations, improve customer engagement and drive profitability. Within this area, there are a number of places to focus:

### Omni-Channel

Ensure all 1st party Otter sales channels achieve feature parity and create differentiated experiences when used together. Look for future opportunities to expand further. This may include looking into progressive web applications or even branded mobile apps.

### Unified Data Layer

Ensure that data seamlessly flows between all 3rd party and first party channels. Ensure the unification of the Otter Eater Profile and Otter Loyalty (as well as third party loyalty where possible). Extend Otter into logical adjacencies like inventory management. We also need to ensure we facilitate the workflows to capture data to allow future platform enhancements.

### Create End Customer Value

As customers become more value conscious it's important to create opportunities for restauranteurs to create value with their end customers. This includes ensuring a true omnichannel loyalty offering, ensure robust discount tooling across all channels and delivering marketing to customers. There are more AI driven opportunities as well which will be discussed in a subsequent section.

## AI-Driven Insights

By taking advantage of our core offering with the Unified Commerce Platform we can further create a competitive advantage by moving beyond reporting into insights. Customers have key metrics that they keep an eye on but it's a reactive checking that brings them to them. Instead there is a large opportunity to move towards notifications and anomaly detection based workflows that surface actionable insights in a timely manner.

### Operational Efficiency:

* Leverage device data to Identify bottlenecks in kitchen operations or service processes.  
  * Drive through wait time  
  * Station dwell time using KDS data  
* Recommend process improvements based on efficiency analysis.  
* AI Based phone and drive through ordering

### Fraud Detection:

* Flag suspicious cashier activities to identify cash skimming  
  * Orders created, sent to kitchen and never closed  
  * Excess voids  
  * Orders created and voided later  
  * Orders created and then after some time modified and completed at a lower price  
* Flag suspicious cashier activities around discount code usage  
  * High discount code usage  
  * Inappropriately applied discounts  
* Flag Manager Code leakage  
  * Manager code used when manager isnt even on duty  
* Flag potential refund, loyalty or gift card fraud  
  * Loyalty ID being used on more than 3 orders in a day  
    * Cashier inputting their own loyalty information

### Menu Optimization:

* Analyze menu item performance for profitability, popularity, and preparation time.  
  * Leverage KDS data to dynamically define prep time  
  * Identify items with long preptimes and low sales data  
  *   
* Analyze menu complexity for things like multi-layer nested modifiers and number of end user required interactions (screens/taps/clicks)  
  * Score menu structures for their performance (ease of orderability) on our various first party platforms.  
* Suggest menu changes or pricing adjustments to maximize revenue and minimize costs.  
  * Surge pricing or dynamic discounts to drive performance and smooth out peaks and valleys on order volume. Can be a benefit of being part of the loyalty program. Guaranteed pricing or also more targeted discounts.  
* Recommend ideal product mix to balance profitability and customer satisfaction.  
  * Reduce modifiers if they are low performing.  
  * Identify potential other modifiers that are doing well and add them across menu items  
  * Identify custom ordered item trends and if they should be added to the menu.

* Predict daily, weekly, and seasonal demand fluctuations.  
* Inform purchasing decisions and staff scheduling.

### Customer Behavior Analysis:

* Identify patterns in customer ordering behavior to inform marketing strategies and menu design.  
  * Leverage ecommerce like retargeting to win back customers.  
* Predict customer preferences for personalized recommendations and promotions.  
  * Allow recall of frequent orders

### Labor Management:

* Predict staffing needs based on historical data, weather forecasts, local events, etc.  
* Optimize staff schedules to ensure appropriate coverage during peak times while minimizing overstaffing during slow periods.  
* Suggest task prioritization during different parts of the day.  
* Leverage detailed staff performance data to help schedule  
* Suggest Kiosks usage when it maybe beneficial based on performance data  
* Nobody had showed up and no order has placed alert maybe an employee has no showed

### Cost Management:

* Track and analyze all costs in real-time.  
  *   
* Identify areas of unexpected cost increases or opportunities for savings.  
  * Flag cost anomalies as they appear  
  * Flag early months that may be on track to cost more unexpectedly.  
  * Identify potential other suppliers that may provide the same items for lower costs

### Inventory Management:

* Predict inventory needs based on historical data, seasonality, and upcoming events/scheduled orders.  
* Optimize order quantities to reduce waste and tie up less capital in inventory.  
* Alert managers to potential shortages or overstock situations.

## Loyalty & Marketing

We have seen loyalty be a common request from customers when they are considering a POS they want to either adopt a comparable inhouse loyalty solution or remain on the loyalty platform they are on. We also have seen competitors leverage loyalty as a moat for prevent customers from churning off their platforms. The leverage aggressive terms of service that say they own the customer and customer data making it nearly impossible for a customer to leave with their eaters data. Exiting Q3 our first party loyalty solution should be approaching feature parity with our competitors in terms of signup, sign in and redemptions. The remaining gaps are more around communication and marketing capabilities.
